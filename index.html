Depression Detection System

AI-Based Multimodal Mental Health Monitor

üîç Project Overview

This project presents a smart and accessible depression detection system using three integrated methods: real-time emotion analysis via webcam, DSM-5-based questionnaire, and text input analysis. The system is designed for individuals, especially the elderly and disabled, who need early detection tools for mental health support.

üß† How Our System Works

  • Webcam Emotion Recognition: Facial expressions, eye movements, and posture are monitored live using OpenCV and deep learning. The system tracks micro-expressions and emotion duration for up to 10 minutes.
  • Motion Analysis: Body stillness, head tilt, blinking rate, and lack of engagement are signs of depressive behavior patterns, tracked over time.
  • Questionnaire (DSM-5): The user fills out a clinical survey covering sleep, energy, mood, and suicidal thoughts. Scores are used to assess severity based on PHQ-9/PHQ-8 standards.
  • Text Input Analysis: NLP models evaluate sentiment in typed journal entries or thoughts, detecting keywords associated with sadness, hopelessness, or social withdrawal.
  • Fusion & Decision: All scores are combined using a deep learning fusion model to predict overall depression risk (low / moderate / high).

üìä Visual Output

After detection, the system generates:

  • Emotion timeline graph: tracking happiness, sadness, and anger
  • Questionnaire bar chart: PHQ-9 category scores
  • Text sentiment heatmap
  • Final report with suggestions and resource links

üéØ Final Goals

Our aim is to build a digital mental health assistant that can help detect early signs of depression in individuals who may not realize their symptoms. It empowers users to reflect, seek support, and get professional help when necessary.

Note: This tool is not a replacement for clinical diagnosis. It is a first-level early screening aid.
